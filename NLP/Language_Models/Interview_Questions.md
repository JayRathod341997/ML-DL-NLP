# Language Models - Interview Questions

## Q1: What is a Language Model?

A language model predicts the probability of a sequence of words. It learns the distribution of words in language.

## Q2: What is perplexity?

Perplexity measures how well a probability model predicts a sample. Lower perplexity = better model.

## Q3: What is n-gram?

An n-gram is a contiguous sequence of n items from a text. For example, bigrams: "the cat", "cat sat"

## Q4: What is the difference between GPT and BERT?

- GPT: Generative Pre-trained Transformer (left-to-right)
- BERT: Bidirectional Encoder Representations (bidirectional)

## Q5: What is transfer learning in LMs?

Pre-train on large corpus, then fine-tune on specific task with smaller dataset.

## Q6: What is temperature in generation?

Temperature controls randomness. Higher temperature = more creative/random, lower = more deterministic.
